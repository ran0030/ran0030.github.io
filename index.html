INFORMATION

Why this investigation is important
Public transport is a major part of daily life in Victoria and reflects how people move, work and study. The COVID-19 pandemic caused a major disruption to travel patterns, especially in metropolitan Melbourne during lockdown periods such as the “ring of steel”. By analysing patronage data from 2018 to 2024, this investigation explores how transport usage changed during the pandemic and how both metropolitan and regional areas recovered over time. Understanding these changes is important for future transport planning and for evaluating how effective past restrictions were.

INFORMATION

What this investigation includes
This investigation looks at how public transport usage in Victoria changed between 2018 and 2024, especially during and after the COVID-19 pandemic. It analyses trends in both metropolitan and regional areas to understand the scale of decline and the pattern of recovery.

It includes:
  • Analysis of monthly and annual patronage trends
  • Identification of the largest decline and recovery levels
  • Comparison between metropolitan and regional services
  • Use of boxplots to examine distribution and variability
  • Analysis and evaluation of COVID-19 impacts
