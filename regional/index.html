Regional Services:
Patronage and Trends Analysis

from 2018 - 2024

SECTION 1:

Overview of Regional Transport
Intro to the Data
This section analyses total regional public transport patronage in Victoria from 2018 to 2024. The data includes regional trains, regional coaches and regional buses operating across regional Victoria. By looking at total yearly patronage, we can see how usage patterns changed before, during and after COVID-19, and how regional trends compare with metropolitan Melbourne.
Analysis
From 2018 to 2019, regional patronage remained relatively stable, reflecting consistent travel between regional centres and Melbourne as well as local movement within regional communities. However, in 2020 there was a clear decline. Regional patronage fell by approximately 51.26% between 2019 and 2020, representing a decrease of over 18 million trips.

Although this decline was substantial, it was smaller than the metropolitan decrease. This suggests regional travel demand may have been less dependent on CBD commuting and more supported by essential travel needs, including work, services and longer-distance connections that are harder to replace.

From 2021 onwards, regional usage began to recover steadily. By 2024, regional patronage had not only recovered but exceeded pre-pandemic levels. This indicates stronger long-term recovery in regional Victoria compared to metropolitan Melbourne, potentially linked to changes in travel patterns and growth in regional demand.

SECTION 2:

Regional Mode Breakdown (Train vs Coach vs Bus)
Intro to the Data
This graph compares total yearly patronage for regional trains, regional coaches and regional buses from 2018 to 2024. Breaking the data down by mode helps identify which services carry the most passengers and how each mode was affected by COVID-19 disruptions and the recovery period.
Analysis
Across the dataset, regional trains consistently record the highest patronage. This reflects the role of V/Line rail services in connecting large regional population centres to Melbourne and offering high capacity for longer-distance travel.

Regional buses also contribute a significant share of regional travel, while coaches generally record the lowest totals. This is expected due to lower service frequency, smaller passenger capacity and reduced demand outside major corridors.

During 2020 and 2021, all modes experienced declines. However, by 2024, the overall regional network shows strong recovery, with total patronage exceeding 2019 levels. This suggests regional transport demand returned more strongly than metropolitan services and may have grown beyond pre-pandemic patterns.

Monthly Trends and Seasonal Patterns
Intro to the Data

Analysis
The monthly regional data shows seasonal variation in pre-pandemic years. Patronage generally fluctuates throughout the year, with quieter periods commonly occurring during holiday months and higher usage during regular school and work periods.

In 2018 and 2019, these patterns are relatively consistent, reflecting stable travel behaviour and predictable demand.

In contrast, 2020 and 2021 show far more irregular movement. Sharp dips occur during periods of stronger restrictions, with changes that are larger than typical seasonal variation. This demonstrates that COVID-19 disruptions strongly affected regional travel, even though the overall decline was less severe than the metropolitan dataset.

From 2022 onwards, the monthly trend becomes more stable again. By 2023 and 2024, the return of more predictable monthly patterns suggests recovery and stabilisation, supporting the broader increase seen in yearly totals.

SECTION 4:

Variability & Distribution Analysis
Intro to the Data
This section looks at how consistent regional transport usage was across each year. Instead of just focusing on totals, it analyses the spread and distribution of monthly data to see how stable travel patterns were before, during and after the pandemic, using a Box Plot chart.
Analysis
Regional transport shows relatively consistent monthly variability in 2018 and 2019. However, in 2020 the distribution shifts downward, with a much lower median and a wider spread between months. This indicates reduced patronage and greater instability during the pandemic disruption period.

From 2021 onwards, the box plot gradually shifts upward, showing ongoing recovery. By 2024, the median monthly patronage rises to approximately 3.14 million, exceeding the 2019 median. The distribution also becomes more stable again, suggesting regional travel patterns recovered strongly and returned to more consistent monthly demand.

Overall, the distribution analysis supports the trend observations, confirming that regional patronage experienced a significant COVID-era decline but recovered more fully than metropolitan transport by 2024.
